# Coffee Sales Analysis – Full Case Study

## 📊 Project Overview
This project analyzes coffee sales data to uncover actionable insights related to product performance, customer behavior, and regional demand. The objective was to transform raw transactional data into an interactive Excel dashboard that enables stakeholders to monitor performance and make data-driven decisions.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem
The business lacked clear visibility into key performance drivers, including:

- Which coffee types and roast categories generate the most revenue
- How customer segments (loyalty vs non-loyalty) impact sales
- Regional performance differences across countries
- Sales trends and seasonal demand patterns

The goal was to identify growth opportunities, optimize product strategy, and improve customer targeting.

---

## 📁 Dataset Description
The dataset consists of transactional sales data, including:

- Order details (Order ID, Order Date)
- Product attributes (Coffee Type, Roast Type, Size)
- Customer data (Customer ID, Loyalty Card status)
- Geographic data (Country)
- Sales metrics (Sales Amount)

The data was initially unstructured and required cleaning and preparation before analysis.

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning & Preparation
Performed in Excel:

- Removed duplicates and handled missing or inconsistent values
- Standardized categorical fields (roast type, size, country)
- Structured data into organized tables for analysis
- Created calculated metrics for aggregation and insights
- Prepared dataset for dashboard integration

---

## 🛠 Tools & Techniques
- Excel → Data cleaning, transformation, and dashboard creation
- Pivot Tables & Charts → Data aggregation and summarization
- Slicers & Timeline Filters → Interactive filtering
- Analytical Techniques → Trend analysis, segmentation, comparative analysis

---

## 🔍 Key Insights (With Metrics)
- The United States contributes the highest share of total sales (~65–70%), significantly outperforming other countries
- A small group of customers (Top 5) contributes ~20–25% of total revenue, indicating high customer concentration
- Arabica coffee shows the most consistent sales performance over time, while other coffee types exhibit higher volatility
- Sales fluctuate over time but peak periodically, with clear seasonal demand patterns across months and years
- Customers with a loyalty card demonstrate higher average order value, indicating stronger engagement and retention
- Medium and Dark roast categories together account for ~60–70% of total sales, reflecting strong customer preference
- 0.2 kg packages account for the highest purchase frequency, indicating a preference for smaller pack sizes and repeat buying behavior

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations
- Focus marketing and expansion efforts on the United States, as it drives the majority of revenue
- Implement targeted retention strategies for high-value customers to maximize lifetime value
- Expand and promote the loyalty program to increase repeat purchases
- Optimize inventory planning for high-demand roast types (Medium & Dark)
- Align packaging strategy with customer behavior by prioritizing smaller pack sizes (0.2 kg)
- Analyze and improve performance in lower-performing regions

---

## 📈 Dashboard Overview
The Excel dashboard provides:

- Sales trends over time segmented by coffee type
- Sales by country for geographic comparison
- Top 5 customers analysis to identify key contributors
- Interactive filters for:
  - Roast Type
  - Size
  - Loyalty Card
  - Time period

The dashboard enables users to explore data dynamically and quickly identify performance trends and business opportunities.
